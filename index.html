How It Works

How Do You Become a Qualified Electrician in the UK?

There are several recognised routes into the electrical industry, but most follow a similar progression structure.

Typical Training Pathway

  • 1 Level 2 Diploma in Electrical Installation
  • 2 Level 3 Diploma in Electrical Installation
  • 3 NVQ Level 3 (workplace competency qualification)
  • 4 AM2 Assessment (final practical assessment)
  • ‚úì ECS Gold Card ‚Äî work as a fully qualified electrician

Electrical Qualifications Explained

Understanding the qualification structure helps you choose the right training path. Each qualification builds on the last, creating a clear route toward full certification and ECS Gold Card status.

This qualification introduces core electrical principles, health and safety, installation practices and theory. It is typically the starting point for new entrants.

The NVQ assesses occupational competence in a real working environment. It is required to become fully qualified and apply for ECS Gold Card status.

The AM2 (Achievement Measurement 2) is the final practical assessment that tests installation, inspection, testing and fault-finding skills under exam conditions.
